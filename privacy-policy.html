Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us. Learn how we protect and handle your personal data.

1. Official Private Company Registration & Authorizations

Meta Software is a privately-owned Egyptian software development company, officially registered with the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology, and an active member of the Information Technology and Communications Industry Chamber. We are licensed by the Investment Authority and authorized by all relevant governmental entities while maintaining our independent private sector status. Our company operates under comprehensive regulatory oversight and maintains all required certifications for providing advanced software solutions throughout the Arab world.

Official Private Company Certifications:

  • Ministry of Communications and Information Technology - Official Private Company Registration
  • Information Technology and Communications Industry Chamber - Active Membership
  • Investment Authority - Private Technology Company License
  • All Relevant Government Entities - Comprehensive Operating Authorizations
  • Private Sector Technology Excellence Certifications
  • Independent Software Development Company Status

2. Introduction - Private Company Privacy Framework

Meta Software, as a comprehensive private software company committed to enhancing practical technological awareness throughout the entire Arab world, is dedicated to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we, as an independent private entity with strong governmental approvals and technical capabilities, collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services.

3. Information We Collect - Private Company Standards

As a private technology company with official governmental approvals, we collect information you provide directly to us through secure channels that comply with private sector standards and regulatory requirements. This includes personal identification information (name, email address, phone number), professional information (company name, job title, industry), technical information (IP address, browser type, device information), usage data (pages visited, time spent, interactions), and project-specific data for our technological awareness initiatives in the Arab world.

4. How We Use Your Information - Private Sector Operations

We use the information we collect to advance technological awareness in the Arab world while maintaining the highest standards of privacy and security. This includes providing, maintaining, and improving our private sector technology services, processing transactions, sending technical notices and updates, responding to inquiries, monitoring usage patterns, enhancing practical technological awareness throughout the Arab world, developing innovative software solutions, supporting digital transformation initiatives, and maintaining compliance with regulatory requirements.

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure - Private Company Policy

As a private company, we do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties without your consent, except as described in this policy. We may share information with trusted private sector service providers, to comply with legal obligations, in connection with business transactions, with your explicit consent, with regulatory authorities when required by law, for industry chamber reporting, to support Arab world technological development initiatives, and with approved private sector partners.

6. Data Security - Private Company Standards

As a private technology company with strong governmental approvals and technical capabilities, we implement robust security measures including advanced encryption of data in transit and at rest, regular security assessments and updates, access controls and authentication measures, employee training on data protection, proprietary security technologies developed in-house, compliance with private sector cybersecurity best practices, regular third-party security audits, investment in cutting-edge security infrastructure, and continuous innovation in data protection technologies.

7. Your Rights Under Egyptian Law

You have comprehensive rights regarding your personal information under Egyptian law, including the right to access your personal data, rectify inaccurate information, erase your personal data, restrict processing, data portability, object to processing, file complaints with Egyptian Data Protection Authority, legal representation in data protection matters, and constitutional rights to privacy and data protection.

8. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies that comply with Egyptian cybersecurity regulations and private sector standards. This includes essential cookies for website functionality, analytics cookies for usage analysis, security cookies for fraud prevention, performance cookies for service optimization, and marketing cookies with appropriate consent mechanisms.

9. International Data Transfers - Government Authorization

Any international data transfers are conducted with appropriate safeguards and regulatory compliance, including prior assessment of data protection adequacy, standard contractual clauses, binding corporate rules, regular monitoring and reporting, and compliance with Egyptian national security requirements.

10. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, with specific retention periods including personal data as required by Egyptian law, financial records for 10 years as mandated by Egyptian Tax Authority, project data as specified in contracts, security logs for 7 years for cybersecurity compliance, and audit trails for regulatory oversight.

11. Children's Privacy - Egyptian Legal Protection

Our services are not intended for children under 16 years of age, and we implement government-approved age verification systems, parental consent mechanisms compliant with Egyptian law, immediate deletion of underage user data when discovered, and reporting to relevant child protection authorities when required.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time with regulatory review by Egyptian authorities, legal compliance verification, public notification through approved channels, and 30-day advance notice to users and authorities.

13. Contact Us

For all privacy-related inquiries, please contact us at privacy@metasoftware.eg, government@metasoftware.eg for government relations, legal@metasoftware.eg for legal matters, compliance@metasoftware.eg for compliance issues, or call +20 123 456 7890. Our registered address is Cairo, Egypt.

14. Data Protection Officer

Our designated Data Protection Officer oversees all data protection activities, ensures compliance with Egyptian and international data protection laws, serves as the primary contact for data protection inquiries, conducts regular privacy impact assessments, manages data breach response procedures, and provides ongoing training to staff on data protection best practices.

15. Third-Party Services

We carefully vet all third-party service providers to ensure they meet our strict data protection standards. All third parties must sign comprehensive data processing agreements, undergo regular security audits, maintain equivalent protection levels, and comply with our data protection requirements.

16. Data Breach Procedures

In the event of a data breach, we have established procedures including immediate containment and assessment, notification to relevant authorities within 72 hours, communication to affected users when required, investigation and remediation, documentation of the incident, and implementation of additional safeguards to prevent future breaches.

17. User Responsibilities

Users are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of their account credentials, using strong passwords and enabling two-factor authentication when available, keeping their contact information up to date, reporting any suspicious activity or security concerns, and complying with our terms of service and acceptable use policies.

18. Data Access and Correction

You have the right to access your personal data held by us and request corrections to any inaccurate or incomplete information. We provide user-friendly mechanisms for accessing and updating personal information, respond to access requests within 30 days, and maintain audit trails of all data access and modification activities.

19. Data Portability

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. We provide data export tools that allow you to download your information, assist with data transfers to other services when requested, and ensure data portability rights are respected while maintaining security standards.

20. Data Deletion

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data under certain circumstances. We provide clear procedures for data deletion requests, ensure secure deletion of data from all systems and backups, maintain records of deletion activities for compliance purposes, and respect legal retention requirements.

21. Data Security Measures

We implement comprehensive security measures including encryption at rest and in transit, multi-factor authentication, regular security assessments, access controls and monitoring, incident response procedures, employee security training, secure development practices, and continuous monitoring for threats and vulnerabilities.

22. Use of Personal Data

We use personal data only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes including service provision, customer support, legal compliance, security monitoring, service improvement, communication with users, and fulfillment of contractual obligations. We do not use personal data for purposes incompatible with the original collection purpose.

23. Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data based on legitimate legal grounds including user consent, contractual necessity, legal obligations, vital interests protection, public task performance, and legitimate interests. We clearly identify the legal basis for each processing activity and respect the specific requirements associated with each basis.

24. Data Minimization

We adhere to the principle of data minimization by collecting only the personal data that is necessary for the specified purposes, regularly reviewing data collection practices, deleting unnecessary data, and implementing privacy-by-design principles in all our systems and processes.

25. Data Accuracy

We take reasonable steps to ensure that personal data is accurate, complete, and up-to-date. This includes providing mechanisms for users to update their information, implementing data validation procedures, regularly reviewing and updating data, and correcting inaccuracies when identified.

26. Data Storage

Personal data is stored in secure, access-controlled environments with appropriate physical and technical safeguards. We use encrypted storage systems, implement backup and recovery procedures, maintain data integrity controls, and ensure storage locations comply with applicable data protection laws.

27. Data Transfer

When transferring personal data, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place including encryption during transmission, secure transfer protocols, verification of recipient security measures, documentation of transfer activities, and compliance with applicable data protection regulations.

28. Data Processing Locations

We process personal data primarily within Egypt and maintain transparency about processing locations. When processing occurs outside Egypt, we ensure adequate protection levels, implement appropriate safeguards, and comply with data localization requirements where applicable.

29. User Consent

When we rely on consent as the legal basis for processing, we ensure consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. We provide clear consent mechanisms, allow withdrawal of consent at any time, maintain records of consent, and respect user choices regarding their personal data.

30. Automated Decision Making

We are transparent about any automated decision-making processes that may affect users. When such processing occurs, we provide information about the logic involved, the significance and consequences of the processing, and offer opportunities for human review and intervention.

31. Marketing Communications

We obtain appropriate consent for marketing communications, provide clear opt-out mechanisms, respect user preferences regarding communication frequency and content, maintain suppression lists for users who have opted out, and comply with applicable marketing and communications laws.

32. User Rights

We respect and facilitate the exercise of user rights including access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, objection, and rights related to automated decision-making. We provide clear procedures for exercising these rights and respond to requests in a timely manner.

33. Complaints and Disputes

We have established procedures for handling privacy-related complaints and disputes including internal complaint mechanisms, escalation procedures, cooperation with supervisory authorities, and alternative dispute resolution options. Users can file complaints with relevant data protection authorities.

34. Policy Updates

We regularly review and update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, and technological developments. Updates are communicated through our website, email notifications, and other appropriate channels with advance notice when significant changes are made.

35. Data Protection Training

We provide comprehensive data protection training to all employees, contractors, and relevant third parties. Training covers data protection principles, legal requirements, company policies, incident response procedures, and best practices for handling personal data.

36. Data Protection Impact Assessments

We conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for processing activities that pose high risks to individual privacy. DPIAs help us identify and mitigate privacy risks, ensure compliance with data protection laws, and implement appropriate safeguards.

37. Data Retention Periods

We establish specific retention periods for different categories of personal data based on legal requirements, business needs, and privacy principles. Retention schedules are regularly reviewed and updated, and data is securely deleted when retention periods expire.

38. Security Incident Response

We maintain a comprehensive incident response plan that includes procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents. The plan covers incident classification, notification requirements, investigation procedures, and post-incident review and improvement processes.

39. Data Encryption

We use strong encryption methods to protect personal data both at rest and in transit. Encryption keys are managed securely, and we regularly review and update our encryption standards to ensure they remain effective against current threats.

40. Access Controls

We implement strict access controls to ensure that personal data is only accessible to authorized individuals who need it for legitimate business purposes. Access controls include user authentication, role-based permissions, regular access reviews, and monitoring of access activities.

41. Data Anonymization

Where appropriate, we use data anonymization techniques to reduce privacy risks while still enabling legitimate uses of data. Anonymization processes are designed to ensure that individuals cannot be re-identified from the anonymized data.

42. Data Backup

We maintain secure backup systems to ensure data availability and business continuity. Backups are encrypted, stored in secure locations, regularly tested for integrity, and subject to the same security controls as primary data systems.

43. Data Recovery

We have established procedures for data recovery in the event of system failures, disasters, or other incidents. Recovery procedures are regularly tested, documented, and updated to ensure they remain effective and meet business continuity requirements.

44. Data Integrity

We implement measures to ensure the integrity and accuracy of personal data including data validation procedures, integrity checks, audit trails, and regular data quality assessments. We promptly correct any data integrity issues that are identified.

45. Data Breach Notification

We have established procedures for notifying relevant authorities and affected individuals in the event of a data breach. Notifications are made within required timeframes and include information about the nature of the breach, potential consequences, and measures taken to address the breach.

46. Privacy by Design

We incorporate privacy considerations into the design and development of all our systems, processes, and services. Privacy by design ensures that privacy protection is built into our operations from the ground up rather than added as an afterthought.

47. Privacy by Default

Our systems and services are configured to provide the highest level of privacy protection by default. Users do not need to take any action to benefit from privacy protections, and privacy-friendly settings are applied automatically.

48. Data Protection Officer Contact

Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at dpo@metasoftware.eg for any privacy-related inquiries, complaints, or requests. The DPO serves as an independent point of contact for data protection matters and works to ensure our compliance with applicable laws.

49. User Authentication

We implement robust user authentication mechanisms to verify user identities and prevent unauthorized access. Authentication methods may include passwords, multi-factor authentication, biometric verification, and other security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of the data and services.

50. Password Policies

51. Two-Factor Authentication

We offer two-factor authentication (2FA) as an additional security layer for user accounts. 2FA requires users to provide a second form of verification in addition to their password, significantly enhancing account security and protecting against unauthorized access.

52. User Account Management

We provide users with tools to manage their accounts including updating personal information, changing privacy settings, reviewing account activity, and deactivating or deleting accounts. Account management features are designed to be user-friendly while maintaining security.

54. Data Sharing with Third Parties
